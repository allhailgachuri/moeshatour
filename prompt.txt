Create a complete, spectacular, premium travel and safari website called moeshatour for Safari Market Tours. The goal is to rebuild the entire project into a world-class, highly polished, responsive, and conversion-focused digital experience that feels like a luxury travel brand rather than a generic brochure site.

Use the current project as a reference point, but do not preserve the old visual weakness. The new output must feel intentional, cinematic, editorial, and professionally engineered. The site should look like a real product a travel company could launch and confidently present to clients, partners, and investors.

Project identity:

- Project name: moeshatour
- Brand: Safari Market Tours
- Git repository: https://github.com/allhailgachuri/moeshatour.git
- Primary purpose: showcase safari tours, destination itineraries, car hire, missionary travel, gallery visuals, and trip booking/inquiry workflows
- Business context: Nairobi-based travel and safari services in Kenya and Tanzania

Overall objective:

- Recreate the entire website from scratch in a way that feels premium, elegant, and highly responsive.
- Make the site feel like a real-world game changer that solves actual trip planning and travel coordination problems.
- Ensure the presentation is deep, clear, trustworthy, and commercially strong.
- Build a visual language that feels cinematic and luxurious without becoming impractical or cluttered.
- Prioritize clarity, brand confidence, high-end aesthetics, usability, and conversion.

Design direction:

- Use inspiration from fluid.glass for glassmorphism, depth, translucency, blur, layered surfaces, and premium motion treatment.
- Use inspiration from johnbowtoursandtravel.com, stejostours.com, and bonfireadventures.com for travel presentation, service clarity, destination storytelling, and practical booking flow.
- Make the site feel intentionally designed, not template-driven.
- Avoid generic AI layout patterns.
- Avoid bland corporate travel styling.
- Avoid overused purple-heavy aesthetics.
- Use warm, earthy, travel-appropriate tones, or another highly deliberate palette that feels premium and coherent.
- Use strong typography contrast, polished spacing, and carefully considered image presentation.
- Make every container, card, and section feel like part of one design system.

Mandatory qualities:

- Extremely responsive on mobile, tablet, and desktop.
- Highly polished visual hierarchy.
- Clear brand storytelling.
- Strong trust signals.
- Beautiful section transitions.
- Elegant cards and sections.
- Accessible semantics and readable contrast.
- Clean navigation.
- Professional motion and hover interaction.
- Cohesive content architecture.

Favicon requirement:

- Use the logo.png file from the project as the favicon.
- Ensure the favicon is set properly in the document head.

What the site must communicate:

- Safari Market Tours creates memorable East African travel experiences.
- The company offers safari packages, mountain climbs, destination travel, car hire, gallery proof, and missionary work support.
- The business should feel established, helpful, and confident.
- The user should understand immediately what the company does and why it is worth trusting.

Recommended content structure:

1. Hero section
- Full-bleed cinematic hero image.
- Strong brand headline.
- Short one-line purpose statement.
- Clear call-to-action buttons.
- Supporting trust copy or key stats.
- Elegant overlay and premium atmospheric treatment.

2. Service or value proposition section
- Explain the key things the company does.
- Include booking support, local guidance, destination choice, transport, and purpose-led travel.
- Present the content as premium cards or glass panels.

3. Featured journeys section
- Showcase key destination packages.
- Include safari, mountain, and coastal examples.
- Use destination imagery, short descriptions, and clear booking links.
- Make the section feel curated and editorial.

4. Fleet or transport section
- Present the car hire service as a serious part of the travel experience.
- Show vehicles in a polished, responsive grid.
- Explain how transport fits different journey types.

5. Brand story section
- Explain who Safari Market Tours is.
- Include history, operational context, and service philosophy.
- Make the copy feel trustworthy, not overly salesy.

6. Philosophy or manifesto section
- Describe the company’s approach to travel planning.
- Show the difference between generic travel selling and intentional experience design.
- Make it feel bold and premium.

7. Gallery section
- Show visual proof through carefully selected images.
- Keep the layout elegant and uncluttered.
- Use it to reinforce atmosphere and quality.

8. Contact section
- Include a map, form, contact details, and location information.
- Make the inquiry flow clear and honest.
- The form should be easy to use on mobile.

9. Footer
- Include brand identity, supporting links, company details, and status indicators.
- Make the footer feel integrated into the design rather than appended.

10. Booking or inquiry page
- Build an inquiry-first flow.
- Do not imply a secure payment system if one is not implemented.
- Capture travel dates, party size, inquiry type, and message details.
- Make the flow practical and trustworthy.

Technical direction:

- If building as a static project, use HTML, CSS, and JavaScript with a strong component-like structure.
- If converting to a framework, use React, Next.js, or Nuxt with reusable components.
- Keep content data-driven where possible.
- Use a single consistent design system.
- Keep repeated markup to a minimum.
- Use semantic HTML.
- Make everything fast and responsive.

If using a framework, prefer:

- Next.js or Nuxt for routing and scalability.
- TypeScript for robustness.
- Tailwind CSS or a disciplined design-token system.
- GSAP or a similar animation layer for tasteful motion.
- A data source or content structure for destinations and vehicles.

Animation direction:

- Use subtle, premium motion only.
- Animate hero text and sections with smooth entrance behavior.
- Use hover lifts and magnetic-feeling buttons.
- Use scroll-based reveals sparingly and with discipline.
- Keep motion sophisticated rather than flashy.

- Ensure every section has a purpose.
- Ensure every CTA has a reason to exist.
- Ensure the site can actually be used to understand the brand, explore offerings, and initiate contact.
- Preserve a premium feel from top to bottom.

- Avoid mismatched button styles.

Final execution goal:

- Recreate moeshatour as a spectacular, responsive, professional travel website that combines premium visual design, strong brand storytelling, modern usability, and practical conversion flow.
- Make it feel like a real travel product that could genuinely be launched, marketed, and improved over time.
